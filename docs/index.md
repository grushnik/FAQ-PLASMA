## ðŸ”¬ Plasma fundamentals

### What is gas plasma?
Gas plasma is an ionized gas formed when energy is applied to a neutral gas, creating a mixture of electrons, ions, neutrals, radicals, and excited species that can conduct electricity and drive chemical reactions. Depending on the energy input, it can be cold (non-thermal) or hot (thermal).

### What are examples of plasma?
Natural examples include lightning, auroras, and the Sun. Man-made plasma includes neon lights, arc welding plasma, plasma cutting torches, and microwave or RF plasma used in laboratories and industry.

### How long does plasma live?
Plasma exists only while energy is supplied. Once power is removed, recombination occurs in microseconds to milliseconds, depending on pressure, gas composition, and temperature.

### Why are plasma torches used?
Plasma torches provide extremely energetic, controllable environments that enable reactions and processes impossible with conventional heat or chemistry, such as waste destruction, gas reforming, and advanced material synthesis.

### What types of plasma torches exist?
Common types include:
- Arc plasma torches
- Inductively Coupled Plasma (ICP)
- Microwave plasma torches
- DC and AC industrial plasma systems
- Low-pressure (cold) plasma sources

Cold plasma has energetic electrons but low bulk gas temperature.  
Hot (thermal) plasma operates at thousands of Kelvin and approaches local thermodynamic equilibrium.
